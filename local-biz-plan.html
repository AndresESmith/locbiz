01
Weeks 1–2 · Foundation

BUILD YOUR SKILLS & PORTFOLIO

Time investment: ~15 hrs/week
🛠

Use Webflow or Framer (not code)

At 15, you don't need to code from scratch. Webflow and Framer let you build professional, custom-looking sites fast. Clients can't tell the difference — and you can still charge premium prices.

🎨

Learn Figma for mockups

Before you build, show clients a Figma mockup. This signals professionalism and massively reduces revisions. Figma is free and has hundreds of tutorials on YouTube.

📁

Build 2–3 fake portfolio sites

Pick local business types (barbershop, bakery, plumber) and build fictional sites for them. These become your portfolio. Name them clearly as "concept designs."

🌐

Your own portfolio site

Build yourname.com using Webflow or Carrd. Keep it clean: your name, niche ("websites for local businesses"), 3 portfolio pieces, and a contact form.

⚡ The shortcut most people miss

Find a local business with a terrible or nonexistent website. Redesign it for free (or $150) to put in your portfolio. One real client project > ten fake ones. Ask a family friend's business, a neighbor's shop, or a local restaurant.

⚠ Legal stuff at 15 (important)

You cannot legally sign contracts on your own in most places. Have a parent co-sign. Use a simple one-page contract (search "freelance web design contract template") — it protects both sides. Always collect 50% upfront before starting any work. Use PayPal, Venmo Business, or Cash App for payments.

💳

Collect 50% upfront, always

Never start work without a deposit. This filters out non-serious clients and ensures you get paid even if the project falls through.

📄

Use a simple contract

Spell out: scope of work, number of revisions, payment schedule, what happens if they ghost. HelloSign or Docusign let you do this free.

🔁

Push retainers hard

Recurring monthly income is the goal. Even 5 clients at $150/mo = $750 passive-ish income on top of new projects.

03
Month 1–2 · Outreach

LAND YOUR FIRST PAYING CLIENTS

The most important phase — execution is everything

Your age is actually an advantage here. Business owners are impressed by a motivated teenager. Lead with it.

  1. Walk into 5 local businesses per week that look like they have a bad or no website. Bring your phone to show your portfolio. Restaurants, salons, auto shops, contractors, and dentists are gold.
  2. Search Google Maps for local businesses with 0 or 1 star reviews that mention "can't find their website" — or no website at all. That's a paid lead.
  3. Message every family friend, parent's contact, or community member. "I build websites for local businesses — do you know anyone who needs one?" One warm referral is worth 100 cold calls.
  4. Post in local Facebook groups and Nextdoor: "I'm a 15-year-old building websites for local businesses — here's my work. First client this month gets 20% off."
  5. Email outreach: Find business emails (usually on Google Maps), send a short 4-sentence email. No walls of text.
📞 Walk-in script (say this out loud)
"Hi — my name's [Name], I'm a local student and I design websites for small businesses. I noticed you don't have a site yet, and I put together a quick concept for you — would you have 2 minutes to take a look? I've already done a few sites in the area and I'd love to help you get found online."
📧 Cold email script (4 sentences max)
"Hi [Name], I noticed [Business] doesn't have a website yet. I'm a local web designer and I've built sites for a few businesses in [Town] — I'd love to do the same for you. I put together a quick mockup of what it could look like [attach screenshot of your fake portfolio piece for their industry]. Would you be open to a 10-minute call this week?"
04
Month 2–3 · Scale

GROW WITHOUT BURNING OUT

Systems > hustle
🔄

Referral machine

After every completed project, ask: "Do you know 2 other business owners who might need a website?" Offer $50–100 referral commission. This becomes your #1 source of leads.

📸

Document everything

Post before/after screenshots of client websites on Instagram and TikTok. "I rebuilt this barbershop's website for $500" gets massive organic reach. Your social proof compounds over time.

⭐

Google reviews = gold

Ask every client to leave a Google review for your "business." Even without an LLC, you can have a Google Business profile. 5-star reviews from real local businesses close future clients instantly.

📦

Add-on services

Once you have clients, upsell: Google Business Profile setup ($150), logo design ($100–200 via Canva or Looka), monthly SEO reports ($100/mo), social media graphics.

The X factor

WHAT SEPARATES THE ONES WHO MAKE IT


Your age is the pitch

Don't hide it — lead with it. "I'm 15 and I built this" makes people want to help you succeed.

Talk to 10 people before expecting 1 yes

Outreach is a numbers game. Most people won't reply. That's normal — keep moving.

Finish what you start

One delivered project on time is worth more than three projects that stall. Reputation is everything in a local market.

Raise prices every 3 clients

Your first site is $400. By your 10th, you should be charging $1,000+. Your confidence and speed both increase.

Never stop the outreach flywheel

Even when you're busy with projects, spend 30 minutes a week finding new leads. The pipeline should never go dry.

Over-communicate with clients

Send weekly progress updates even if there's little to report. Clients who feel informed stay happy and refer others.
