📌 The psychology of three tiers

When you show three options, most people choose the middle one. The top tier makes the middle seem reasonable; the bottom tier makes it seem like a deal. Name your packages clearly — never say "Option A, B, C." Names like Starter / Growth / Pro signal a progression clients understand.

⚠ Never quote hourly as a beginner

Hourly pricing penalizes you for getting faster. A site that takes you 20 hours now will take you 8 hours in 2 months — you'd earn less money for the same result. Always quote project-based prices. As you get faster, your effective hourly rate goes up automatically.

Step 2 of 4 — Recurring Income

The Retainer Model

Monthly retainers are how you turn one-time projects into stable recurring income. Offer this to every client after delivery.

The Monthly Math

5 retainer clients × $150/mo $750/mo
2 new project sites × $800 avg $1,600/mo
1 upsell (logo, SEO, Google setup) $200/mo
Monthly total (conservative) $2,550/mo
✓ What to include in a $150/mo retainer
  • Up to 2 hours of site updates per month (text/photo changes, new menu, etc.)
  • Hosting management (you manage the Webflow/Framer subscription, they reimburse you)
  • Monthly check-in message to confirm all is running well
  • Priority response within 24 hours if anything breaks
  • Monthly performance summary (Google Analytics screenshot)
💬 How to pitch the retainer

"Most business owners don't want to touch their website themselves, and I don't blame them. For $150/month I'll handle all updates, make sure nothing breaks, and keep everything current — so you never have to think about it. A lot of my clients consider it the best $150 they spend."

Step 3 of 4 — Legal Protection

Your Contract & Legal Setup

At 15, you can't legally sign contracts in most places — but your parent can co-sign. This section tells you exactly what needs to be in every contract.

⚠ The #1 thing most teen freelancers skip (don't)

A verbal agreement protects nobody. Even a simple one-page document signed by both parties (and your parent) makes clients take you seriously, eliminates misunderstandings, and gives you legal recourse if they don't pay. Get a contract — every single time.

Clause What to write / why it matters
Required

Scope of Work 		List exactly what you're building: "A 3-page Webflow website including Home, Services, and Contact. Includes contact form, mobile optimization, and 1 round of revisions." The more specific, the better. This is your protection against "can you also add a blog, shop, and 10 more pages?"
Required

Payment Terms 		50% deposit before work begins, 50% on delivery before transfer of site access. Write this explicitly. "Work begins upon receipt of $[X]. Remaining $[X] is due upon project completion before final files/access are transferred."
Required

Revision Policy 		State how many rounds of revisions are included and what counts as a revision. "Contract includes [X] rounds of revisions. A revision round = one consolidated set of feedback. Additional revisions billed at $75/hr." Without this, clients will revise forever.
Required

Timeline 		Include your estimated delivery timeline AND a clause requiring the client to provide content (text, photos, etc.) within 5 business days. If they delay providing content, the timeline extends. Clients who don't send content are the #1 cause of delayed projects.
Ownership Upon final payment, the client owns the website design. You retain the right to show it in your portfolio. You do NOT need to give them source files (Figma, etc.) unless explicitly agreed — just the live website.
Kill Fee If they cancel mid-project, they keep the deposit. This prevents clients from having you do 80% of the work and then backing out. Add: "If project is cancelled after work has begun, the deposit is non-refundable."
Parent Co-signature Add a line: "This contract is co-signed by [Parent Name], parent/guardian of [Your Name], and constitutes a binding agreement." This makes the contract fully enforceable.
📎 Where to get a free template
  • Bonsai (bonsai.io) — free freelance contract template, very detailed
  • AND.CO — another free contract generator
  • Search "freelance web design contract template PDF" — customize as needed
  • Use DocuSign or HelloSign (free plan) to sign digitally — no printing needed

⚠ Tax note for your parents

If you earn over ~$400/year freelancing in the US, it's technically taxable income. Have your parents aware of this. Keep a simple spreadsheet of all income and expenses. You won't owe much (if anything) at these early income levels, but being organized now prevents headaches later. A parent can help you file if needed.
