Step 1 of 5 — Systems

Build Systems, Not Just Work

The difference between a freelancer who burns out and one who scales is systems. These four tools take under 2 hours to set up and save you hours every week.

01

Client Pipeline Tracker

A simple spreadsheet or Notion board tracking every lead and client: Lead → Contacted → Proposal Sent → Closed → In Progress → Delivered → Retainer. Review every Friday. You should always know where every client conversation stands.

Tool: Notion (free)
02

Project Template Kit

Create a reusable Webflow/Framer template for each business type (salon, restaurant, contractor). Each new project starts from a template — saves 4–6 hours per build. Over 10 projects, this saves you 50+ hours.

Tool: Webflow / Framer
03

Onboarding Questionnaire

Before starting any project, send clients a Google Form asking: business name, services, target customer, color preferences, competitors, any text/photos they have. This replaces 3–4 back-and-forth emails and gets you the content you need upfront.

Tool: Google Forms (free)
04

Automated Invoicing

Set up Wave to auto-send invoices and payment reminders. When a project is complete, you trigger the invoice — Wave handles follow-up reminders automatically so you don't have to awkwardly chase payments.

Tool: Wave (free)
✓ The onboarding questionnaire saves the most time

The biggest time sink in web projects isn't building — it's waiting for clients to send you their logo, their text, their photos. A mandatory upfront form with a clear deadline ("please complete this within 5 days of signing") eliminates the most common project delay.

Step 2 of 5 — Upsells

Upsell Services to Every Client

After delivering a site, most clients are happy and receptive to add-ons. These are easy to deliver and significantly increase your revenue per client.

Service Price How to deliver it
Google Business Profile Setup $150 Create or claim their Google Business listing, add photos, hours, services, and a link to the new website. Takes ~45 minutes. Every local business needs this — it's how they show up on Google Maps.
Logo Design $100–200 Use Looka ($20 AI tool) or Canva to create a clean logo. Present 3 options. Charge $100–200 and pocket the difference. Most small businesses have no logo or a terrible one. Tool: Looka, Canva
Monthly SEO Report $100/mo Set up Google Search Console (free) for their site. Every month, screenshot their rankings and traffic. Write 3 bullet points of what improved. Send in an email. Takes 15 minutes — charges $100. Tool: Google Search Console (free)
Social Media Graphics Pack $100–150 Design 10 branded social media post templates in Canva. Client can reuse them forever. Pair with their existing branding and site colors. Easy and quick — most business owners desperately want consistent-looking posts. Tool: Canva (free)
Email Newsletter Setup $200 one-time Set up Mailchimp (free) for the client. Create a signup form, add it to their site, and design one email template. Show them how to send one. Takes ~2 hours total. Huge perceived value. Tool: Mailchimp (free)
Annual Site Refresh $200–400 After 12 months, offer to update photos, copy, and design details. Most businesses have changed and their site is already outdated. Easy recurring revenue from existing clients.
💬 How to pitch upsells naturally

Pitch right after delivery, when they're most excited: "Your site is live and looking great. One thing I noticed is you don't have a Google Business Profile set up yet — that's how you show up on Google Maps. I can handle that for $150, usually done within a day. Want me to take care of it?"

Step 3 of 5 — Pricing Growth

Raise Your Prices Consistently

Your prices should increase every few clients. Here's the timeline to follow. The confidence to charge more comes from more deliveries — not from waiting until you "feel ready."

Client 1 (the free/cheap one)
Free–$200 Portfolio builder

This is a learning experience, not a payday. Do excellent work, collect a testimonial, and use it to justify real prices.

Clients 2–3
$400–500 Starter rate

Introductory pricing. You're getting reps in, learning client communication, and building more portfolio pieces. Still worth it at this rate.

Clients 4–6
$600–800 Growing confidence

You've delivered 5+ real projects. Raise to $600 minimum. You now have real testimonials, real portfolio pieces, and real experience to justify this.

Clients 7–12
$900–1,200 Professional rate

By now you have a proven track record. Raise to $1,000 for a standard 5-page site. If clients push back, show your portfolio and let the work speak. Some will say no — that's okay. The ones who say yes subsidize the ones who don't.

Month 6+
$1,500+ Agency territory

At this point you have testimonials, a strong portfolio, referrals, and systems. You're operating better than most adult freelancers. Premium pricing is completely justified — and some clients prefer paying more because it signals professionalism.

Step 4 of 5 — Referrals

Build Your Referral Engine

After Phase 3, referrals should become your #1 source of clients. Referred clients trust you before they meet you, close faster, and pay more without negotiating.

01

Deliver outstanding work

Over-communicate. Deliver on time. Show the client something beautiful.

02

Ask for a testimonial

"Would you mind writing 2–3 sentences about your experience? I'd love to share it on my site."

03

Ask for referrals directly

"Do you know 2–3 other business owners who might need a site? I'd offer you $75 for any intro that becomes a client."

04

Pay the referral reward

When they send a client who pays, send $75 immediately. Word spreads fast when people get paid for referrals.

✓ The referral math

If each client refers just 1 new client, your client base doubles with every project cycle. 5 clients become 10. 10 become 20. You pay $75 per referral and earn $800–1,500+ per project. That's a 10–20x return on referral spend.

Step 4.5 of 5 — Social Proof

Document & Post Your Work

Social media compounds over time. One post about "I rebuilt this barbershop's website at 15" can get thousands of views and generate inbound leads for months.

TikTok / Reels 1–2x/week

Content ideas

  • "I built this restaurant a website for $800" (before/after)
  • "How I got my first client at 15"
  • "Day in my life as a 15-year-old freelancer"
  • "Watch me build a website in 30 minutes" (timelapse)
  • "How much I made this week"
Instagram 2–3x/week

Content ideas

  • Before/after screenshots of client sites
  • Screenshot of a client text saying "love it!"
  • Your desk/workspace setup
  • Carousel: "5 signs a local business needs a new website"
  • Milestone posts: "Just hit $1k in sales at 15"
LinkedIn 1x/week

Content ideas

  • Written posts: "What I've learned from my first 5 freelance clients"
  • Case study: "How I helped [type] increase bookings with a new site"
  • Professional introduction post (gets major engagement at 15)
  • Lessons from pricing mistakes
⚠ Always get client permission before posting

Before sharing any client work publicly, ask: "Would you mind if I shared your site on my social media as a portfolio example?" Most say yes immediately — it's free marketing for them too. If they say no, respect it and never post their work.
